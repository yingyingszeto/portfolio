Digital Office IA Redesign

Insurance | B2B

The Hong Kong Market of Manulife (John Hancock) is one of the largest insurance services in the region However, their sales agents were still using a 20-year-old legacy workplace system that needed updating to better support their workflow. This initiative also provided an opportunity to align with company's new design system. Our first priority was to redesign the information architecture (IA).


Website

My Role

I worked as a UX designer in close collaboration with a researcher, product owners, business analysts and engineering leads.

Problem

The legacy system had a flat IA hierarchy where everything was immediately visible. As new functions were added over time, each top menu accumulated more than 20 subcategories. This made it difficult to find information efficiently. Experienced agents relied on memory to navigate the system, while new joiners struggled to learn how to use it.

Outcome

Research

Findings revealed that agents had to frequently switch between multiple menus to complete a single task, with content scattered throughout the system where some sub-menus only acted as shortcuts to specific levels. Data showed that 30% of content had no or minimal user traffic, with agents only accessing certain functions frequently.

Menu Restructuring & New Navigation Patterns

To restructure the menu system, we mapped agents' daily workflows through "Day in the Life of Agent" exercise and consolidated tasks into different "work modes" based on contextual use.

We refined labels to use familiar terminology and implemented in-page navigation for related tasks, creating a shallower hierarchy that reduced cognitive load while preventing excessive menu switching.

Tree Testing

With over 300 responses, we used the data to evaluate whether the initial IA aligned with agents' mental models. Our primary goal was to assess how agents perceived the labeling of the top menus.

For example, we found that agents might view the "Lead" generation function as part of "Customer" management. Given that the function's primary purpose was creating new sales opportunities, we kept it under the "Sales" menu while making it accessible from the client list in the "Customer" menu.

Going Forwards

The IA proposal was just the beginning of the digital office revamp. It successfully moved the project to the next stage—securing funding, planning resources, and coordinating change management activities with the operation team. Our next steps were:

  • Concept Testing on key user flows
  • Content mapping with content owners and the technical team
  • Glossary and Terminology documentation
  • Page templates and design components
